Explanation of Metrics
  • Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV): The predicted revenue a customer will generate over their entire relationship with your business.
  • Average Customer Lifespan: The average duration a customer remains active, in years.
  • Total Revenue: The total revenue generated by all customers during the specified time interval.
  • Number of Purchases: The total number of purchases made by all customers during the specified time interval.
  • Number of Customers: The total number of unique customers.
  • Average Purchase Value (APV): The average value of each purchase. Calculated as Total Revenue divided by Number of Purchases.
  • Average Purchase Frequency Rate (APFR): The average number of purchases per customer. Calculated as Number of Purchases divided by Number of Customers.
  • Customer Value (CV): The average revenue generated per customer. Calculated as APV multiplied by APFR.
  • Time Interval: The time span between the earliest start date and the latest end date in the dataset.
  • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): The amount the company can afford to spend on acquiring a new customer. Typically, this is a percentage of the CLTV. In this case, we assume the company can spend up to 30% of the CLTV.
